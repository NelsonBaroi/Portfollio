Why I'm Telling This Story

I'm not a celebrity, politician, or talking head. I'm a professional from Chandraghona in the Chittagong Hill Tracts who — through a government scholarship, learning Russian the hard way, and a lot of deliberate work — ended up directing operations on Bangladesh's first nuclear power plant.

Now, at what looks like the peak of that career, I'm pressing pause. Not to escape. To sharpen. In September 2026 I start an MSc in Business Analytics at ATU Galway, Ireland.

This page is the longer version of my story. Roles, dates, and skills live on my portfolio — I kept those separate so this doesn't repeat them.

Where I'm From

The Geography of Home

I was born and raised in Chandraghona, a small town nestled in the Rangamati District of Bangladesh's Chittagong Hill Tracts (CHT). The CHT is a region unlike any other in Bangladesh — a hilly, forested landscape bordering Myanmar and India, home to indigenous tribal communities and ethnic minorities. It is a place of extraordinary natural beauty, but also of historical complexity, ethnic tension, and economic marginality.

Chandraghona itself sits along the Karnaphuli River, approximately 50 kilometres from the port city of Chittagong. The town is perhaps best known for the Karnaphuli Paper Mills, once the largest paper mill in South Asia, which historically shaped the local economy and community identity.

A Minority Within a Minority

I belong to the Christian minority community of Bangladesh. In a country where over 90% of the population is Muslim, Christians represent roughly 0.4% of the total population. Being a Christian from the Chittagong Hill Tracts puts me at a unique intersection of identities — ethnically distinct, religiously minority, and geographically peripheral.

That background shaped me — the quiet resilience, the belief that merit and education are how you move, and being comfortable as "the different one" in a room — whether that room is a Russian university lecture hall or a nuclear power plant construction site managed by international teams.

Early Character Formation

Growing up in Chandraghona gave me the values I still live by: integrity, hard work, and empathy. My family was supportive and invested in my development. The combination of a tight-knit minority community and the natural challenges of growing up in a less-developed region created the conditions for what became my defining trait: the ability to extract maximum value from limited resources.

School Years

SSC: Chandraghona, Rangamati (Completed 2009)

I completed my Secondary School Certificate (SSC) examinations in 2009 at a school in Chandraghona, Rangamati. The SSC is the first major public examination in the Bangladeshi education system, typically taken at age 15-16. Finishing SSC in the Hill Tracts — far from Dhaka's feeder schools — and still getting into Notre Dame told me early that merit could open doors.

HSC: Notre Dame College, Dhaka (Completed 2011)

For my Higher Secondary Certificate (HSC), I moved to Dhaka city to attend Notre Dame College, Dhaka — widely considered the most prestigious higher secondary institution in Bangladesh.

About Notre Dame College

Notre Dame College was established in 1949 by the Congregation of Holy Cross, making it one of the oldest Catholic educational institutions in Bangladesh. It has consistently been ranked as the premier college in the country for intermediate education. The college is known for:

  • Extremely competitive admissions (acceptance rates among the lowest in the country)
  • Outstanding board examination results
  • A tradition of producing leaders in science, business, medicine, and public service
  • A campus in Motijheel, the commercial heart of Dhaka

Getting into Notre Dame from Chandraghona was a big deal. It took more than grades — leaving home at sixteen and learning Dhaka on my own.

The Notre Dame Experience

Notre Dame (2009–2011) threw me into a dramatically different environment from my hometown: urban, intellectually competitive, diverse (Notre Dame accepts students of all faiths), and rigorous. The college's Holy Cross ethos — emphasising service, integrity, and intellectual formation — matched what my family had taught me, and it stuck.

The connection to the Holy Cross community also represents a continuity with my Christian identity in a predominantly Muslim country. Notre Dame provided both academic excellence and a community of shared faith — something particularly valuable for a teenager far from home.

Russia

The Russian Government Scholarship (2013/2014-2018)

After completing my HSC in 2011, I secured a Russian Government Scholarship to study at Kalmyk State University (officially: B.B. Gorodovikov Kalmyk State University) in the Republic of Kalmykia, Russia. This scholarship — offered by the Russian government to international students from developing nations — is highly competitive and covers tuition, accommodation, and a monthly stipend.

About Kalmyk State University

Kalmyk State University is located in Elista, the capital of the Republic of Kalmykia — a federal subject of Russia located in the southern part of the country, between the Caspian Sea and the Black Sea. Kalmykia is notable as the only Buddhist-majority region in Europe, with a predominantly Kalmyk (Mongol) population. The university offers programmes across sciences, humanities, engineering, and technology, and actively recruits international students.

The choice of Kalmykia for a Bangladeshi student is not arbitrary — Russian government scholarships assign students to universities across the federation, and the experience of studying in a culturally unique region (Buddhist, steppe landscape, continental climate) was profoundly formative.

Bachelor's Degree in Business Informatics (2014-2018)

I enrolled in the Faculty of Management and Law (or a related faculty) to pursue a Bachelor's degree in Business Informatics. Business Informatics as a discipline in the Russian higher education system combines:

  • Management science and organisational theory
  • Information systems and computer science
  • Economics and quantitative methods
  • Systems analysis and business process modelling

The programme typically runs 4 years (8 semesters) in the Russian system.

Thesis: "The Use of Computer Technology for Intensification of Business Processes in the Field of Forecasting"

My undergraduate thesis explored the application of computer technology to optimise and accelerate business processes, specifically in the domain of forecasting. This topic sits at the intersection of business process management, information systems, and predictive analytics — foreshadowing my later interest in data analytics and business intelligence.

That thesis "first made me understand how data, properly handled, becomes a strategic instrument rather than just a record." This insight — that data is not merely descriptive but prescriptive and strategic — shaped my career and my decision to pursue an MSc in Business Analytics nearly a decade later.

Rooppur — Bangladesh's Nuclear Bet

Overview of the Project

The Rooppur Nuclear Power Plant (RNPP) is Bangladesh's first nuclear power plant and one of the most significant infrastructure projects in the country's history. Located in Rooppur, Ishwardi Upazila, Pabna District, on the eastern bank of the Padma River (Ganges), approximately 160 kilometres northwest of Dhaka, the plant represents Bangladesh's entry into the nuclear age.

Key Statistics

Parameter Detail
Total Capacity 2,400 MW (2 × 1,200 MW)
Reactor Type VVER-1200 (Generation III+)
Designer/Builder Rosatom (Russia)
Project Value $12.65 billion
Construction Start 2017 (Unit 1 concrete pour: 2018)
Expected Operational 2026 (Unit 1 fuel loading began May 2026)
Significance Makes Bangladesh the world's 33rd nuclear power-producing nation

The "Little Russia" Phenomenon

At its peak, over 20,000 people worked on the Rooppur site. More than 4,500 Russian nationals live and work in a purpose-built residential zone surrounding the plant. The area has transformed from a quiet Padma riverbank village into what locals call "Little Russia" — complete with Russian signage, Russian restaurants, dental clinics serving Russian expats, and Bengali vegetable vendors who have learned to negotiate in Russian.

I'll put it plainly: the once-quiet village has become "a bustling international township." This is the context in which I works daily — a cross-cultural crucible where Bangladeshi bureaucracy meets Russian engineering precision, where local labour forces interface with international nuclear safety standards, and where My trilingual abilities make him not just useful but essential.

The Technology: VVER-1200

The VVER-1200 is a Generation III+ pressurised water reactor, incorporating advanced passive and active safety systems. It meets the most stringent international safety requirements established after Fukushima. The reactor design is Rosatom's flagship export product, also being deployed at the El Dabaa Nuclear Power Plant in Egypt (where Atom Tech Alliance is also involved).

AMT Engineering JSC and Atom Tech Alliance

AMT Engineering JSC is a member of the Atom Tech Alliance group — a Russian engineering conglomerate that supplies auxiliary equipment and engineering services for nuclear power plants worldwide. The group's portfolio extends beyond Rooppur to include projects in Egypt and other locations.

Atom Tech Alliance describes itself as "an engineering company accumulated years of experience in supplying equipment, instruments and technologies for nuclear power plants, petrochemical facilities, gas and coal-fired power plants."

Ireland

The Decision

In what might appear counterintuitive — leaving a Director-level position to become a student again — I applied for and got into the MSc in Business Analytics at Atlantic Technological University (ATU), Galway, Ireland, commencing September 2026.

But the decision is entirely logical for me. Frankly: "My leadership role now demands formal postgraduate-level analytical skills. The MSc in Business Analytics at ATU directly addresses this gap."

Here's the thing: "I can read a report and draw a conclusion. What drives me is the ambition to build the model behind the report, stress-test its assumptions, and communicate its limitations clearly."

This is not a career pivot — it is a career sharpening. Six years of operational leadership showed me exactly where my analytical ceiling was. I'm removing it.

About ATU Galway

Atlantic Technological University (ATU) was formed in 2022 through the merger of Galway-Mayo Institute of Technology (GMIT), Institute of Technology Sligo, and Letterkenny Institute of Technology. The Galway campus is the largest, located in Ireland's cultural capital on the west coast.

ATU's Business Analytics programme is designed for professionals who need practical analytical tools — it is applied rather than purely theoretical, which is what I need — build models, not just study them.

How I Work

How I talk

If you've read my writing or talked to my AI twin, you probably already know this. Still, for the record:

Direct and No-Nonsense

I don't waste words. My AI twin uses "short sentences for points, longer for explanations" — which matches how I actually talk. I say "Look," "Here's the thing," and "Frankly" when it matters.

Warm but Professional

I'm warm, but direct. I won't pad bad news with polite fluff — I'll tell you clearly, without being cruel.

Challenges Assumptions

"Are you sure?" "That doesn't add up." I question received wisdom and expects others to defend their positions with evidence rather than authority. This intellectual combativeness is constructive rather than hostile.

Honest About Uncertainty

"Honestly, I don't know that yet" — I'm fine saying when I don't know. This is rare in professional contexts where many feel compelled to project omniscience. That honesty matters to me.

Decision-Making Framework: OODA Loop

I use the OODA Loop (Observe-Orient-Decide-Act) — a military decision-making framework developed by US Air Force Colonel John Boyd. I run personal decisions through it too — asking "OODA what if I submit only that mentioned part?" when evaluating visa documentation strategy.

I'm strategic — I don't just react to circumstances but systematically processes information through a structured decision framework. The OODA loop is favoured by fighter pilots, chess players, and business strategists. I use it for everything from financial document preparation to career planning.

Planning and Preparation

I plan obsessively. Examples:

  • Built a custom web portal to organise visa documents
  • Created a 52-week study plan broken into weekly modules
  • Researched 10 Galway hotels individually, including typical pay rates, application strategies, and personalised competitive advantage analysis
  • Prepared job application templates, interview Q&A frameworks, and timeline strategies months before arrival
  • Designed and built an AI chatbot to represent me professionally 24/7

This is not anxiety-driven over-preparation. I've learned that preparation compounds — every hour invested in planning saves multiple hours of reactive problem-solving later.

Relationship with Technology

I'm pragmatic about tech. I didn't learn Python for fun — "the market demands it" for business analytics professionals. I built the chatbot because it serves my professional brand.

Technology is a tool in my hands, not who I am. This pragmatism works for me: I choose the right tool for the right job without becoming attached to any particular stack or framework.
