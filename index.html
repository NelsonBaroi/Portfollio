About Me

I'm Nelson Baroi, currently serving as the Director of the Bangladesh Branch at AMT Engineering JSC. My work is part of something larger than any single role — the construction of the Rooppur Nuclear Power Plant, Bangladesh's first nuclear energy project and one of the most significant infrastructure investments in the country's history.

The plant is located at Rooppur in Ishwardi, about 160 kilometres northwest of Dhaka, on the bank of the river Padma. It houses two Russian-designed VVER-1200 reactors — Generation III+ pressurised water reactors with a combined capacity of 2,400 megawatts. The project, valued at $12.65 billion, was built in partnership with Russia's Rosatom and positions Bangladesh as the world's 33rd nuclear power-producing nation.

My journey here wasn't a straight line. It involved moving to Russia at 19, studying in a language I didn't speak, returning to Bangladesh, and gradually building a career in an environment where decisions carry real weight. Every step taught me something about resilience, about working across cultures, and about the kind of leadership that earns trust rather than demands it.

The Project

The Rooppur Nuclear Power Plant is not just an engineering achievement — it's a national turning point. Construction of Unit 1 began in November 2017 and Unit 2 in July 2018. In May 2026, nuclear fuel loading for Unit 1 was successfully completed ahead of schedule, marking Bangladesh's official entry into the nuclear era.

Thousands of engineers — Russian and Bangladeshi — have worked together on this site for years. The coordination between two fundamentally different work cultures, languages, and operational philosophies is one of the project's great unspoken challenges. That's where I operate: at the intersection of strategy, logistics, and people.

As Director of the Bangladesh Branch at AMT Engineering JSC, I oversee budgets, contracts, resource allocation, employee welfare, and safety compliance. But the truth is, the job is really about making sure the right people have the right information at the right time — so that decisions get made properly, even under pressure.

My Background

I grew up in Chandraghona, a small town in the Chittagong Hill Tracts of Bangladesh. My academic path took me to Notre Dame College, Dhaka — one of the country's most respected institutions — and then, on a Russian Government Scholarship, to Kalmyk State University in the Russian Federation, where I studied Business Informatics.

Living and studying in Russia for five years — as the only foreign student in my class — taught me more than any coursework could. I learned to communicate across barriers, to adapt quickly, and to find common ground with people whose lives looked nothing like mine. My thesis explored how computer technology could improve business forecasting — a question I'm still working on, just at a larger scale now.

I speak Bengali, English, and Russian. That combination isn't accidental — it's what allows me to bridge the gap between Russian management and Bangladeshi operations on a daily basis.

Beyond Work

I believe in giving back. During my university years, volunteering and student organisations taught me the value of service. Today, I continue to support initiatives that uplift others — whether it's mentoring younger professionals on the project, advocating for workplace safety standards, or supporting education initiatives in my home community.

I'm also investing in my own growth. I'm currently deepening my skills in SQL, Python, and data analysis — not because I plan to change careers, but because I've seen firsthand that the leaders who make the best decisions are those who can work with data, not just intuition.
