About Me

I'm Nelson Baroi, currently serving as the Director of the Bangladesh Branch at AMT Engineering JSC. My work is part of something larger than any single role — the construction of the Rooppur Nuclear Power Plant, Bangladesh's first nuclear energy project and one of the most significant infrastructure investments in the country's history.

The plant is located at Rooppur in Ishwardi, about 160 kilometres northwest of Dhaka, on the bank of the river Padma. It houses two Russian-designed VVER-1200 reactors — Generation III+ pressurised water reactors with a combined capacity of 2,400 megawatts. The project, valued at $12.65 billion, was built in partnership with Russia's Rosatom and positions Bangladesh as the world's 33rd nuclear power-producing nation.

I began at AMT Engineering in January 2019 as a Senior Administrator, managing internal operations, reporting, and coordination across departments. In February 2024, I was appointed to my current role — responsible for strategic planning, resource allocation, performance evaluation, and cross-departmental decision-making. The journey from administration to directorship wasn't planned from a textbook. It was built through six years of showing up, solving problems no one else wanted, and earning the trust of people who operate under real pressure.

The Project

The Rooppur Nuclear Power Plant is not just an engineering achievement — it's a national turning point. The General Contract was signed on December 25, 2015, and construction of Unit 1 began in November 2017, Unit 2 in July 2018. In May 2026, nuclear fuel loading for Unit 1 was successfully completed ahead of schedule, marking Bangladesh's official entry into the nuclear era as the world's 33rd nuclear power-producing nation.

At its peak, over 20,000 people worked on this site. More than 4,500 Russian nationals live and work here — transforming what was once a quiet village on the banks of the Padma into a bustling international township that locals have come to call "Little Russia." Shop signboards in Russian, new hospitals, restaurants, and residential complexes now line the roads of Ishwardi. The scale of human coordination involved is staggering.

Atom Tech Alliance — the group AMT Engineering JSC belongs to — supplies auxiliary equipment and engineering services for nuclear power plants worldwide, including the El Dabaa NPP in Egypt. At Rooppur, the Rosatom State Corporation's Engineering Division serves as both general designer and general contractor, with AtomEnergoMash supplying reactor island equipment and turbine island components.

The decisions I make daily — on staffing, budget allocation, operational performance, and quarterly planning — require more than intuition. I can read a report and draw a conclusion. What drives me to keep growing is the ambition to build the model behind the report, stress-test its assumptions, and communicate its limitations clearly. That gap between operational instinct and structured analytical thinking is one I work to close every day.

My Background

I grew up in Chandraghona, a small town in the Chittagong Hill Tracts of Bangladesh. My academic path took me to Notre Dame College, Dhaka — one of the country's most respected institutions — and then, on a Russian Government Scholarship, to Kalmyk State University in the Russian Federation, where I studied Business Informatics.

My degree gave me a foundational understanding of the relationship between business operations and information systems. My thesis — "The Use of Computer Technology for Intensification of Business Processes in the Field of Forecasting" — first made me understand how data, properly handled, becomes a strategic instrument rather than just a record. That understanding has only deepened through six years of increasingly senior operational work.

Living and studying in Russia for five years — as the only foreign student in my class — taught me more than any coursework could. I learned to navigate unfamiliar environments, build relationships across cultural divides, and find common ground with people whose lives looked nothing like mine. I speak Bengali, English, and Russian fluently — and that combination is what allows me to bridge the gap between Russian management and Bangladeshi operations every single day.

Beyond Work

I believe in giving back. During my university years, volunteering and student organisations taught me the value of service. Today, I continue to support initiatives that uplift others — whether it's mentoring younger professionals on the project, advocating for workplace safety standards, or supporting education initiatives in my home community.

I'm also investing in my own growth. I'm currently deepening my skills in SQL, Python, and data analysis — not because I plan to change careers, but because I've seen firsthand that the leaders who make the best decisions are those who can work with data, not just intuition.
