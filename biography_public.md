# Background

I'm Nelson Baroi. I grew up in Chandraghona in the Chittagong Hill Tracts, studied in Dhaka and Russia, and for several years I've helped run the Bangladesh side of work at the Rooppur Nuclear Power Plant. In September 2026 I start an **MSc in Business Analytics** at [ATU Galway](https://www.atu.ie).

This page is context — who I am and how I got here. Roles, dates, and skills are on the [home page](index.html). A printable summary is on my [CV](cv.html).

## Where I grew up

Chandraghona is a small town in Rangamati, along the Karnaphuli River. The Hill Tracts are a different Bangladesh from Dhaka — quieter, more remote, and not an obvious launchpad for an international career. My family cared about education and doing honest work. That shaped me more than any job title.

## School and university

- **SSC (2009)** — Chandraghona, Rangamati
- **HSC (2011)** — Notre Dame College, Dhaka
- **BSc Business Informatics (2014–2018)** — Kalmyk State University, Russia (government scholarship)

Leaving home for Notre Dame at sixteen was hard. Russia was harder — Russian-language classes, and I was the only foreign student in mine. I learned to adapt before I learned to manage.

My thesis was on using technology to improve business forecasting. That's still the thread: I want decisions backed by evidence, not just instinct.

## Rooppur — the work I do now

[Rooppur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rooppur_Nuclear_Power_Plant) is Bangladesh's first nuclear power plant — a long, complex build with Russian engineering and Bangladeshi operations on the ground.

I'm **Director of the Bangladesh branch** at [AMT Engineering JSC](https://atom-alliance.com/en/project/constuction/npp-rooppur/). Day to day: budgets, contracts, logistics, and keeping Russian and Bangladeshi teams working from the same page. I use Russian in meetings — it isn't a line on a CV, it's part of the job.

I also support [Freyssinet](https://www.freyssinet.com/) on procurement when the project needs it. Dates and titles are on my [career timeline](index.html#professional-journey).

## Why Ireland

I'm not stepping away from operations because I lost interest. I could read a report and act on it — but I wanted to learn how to build and stress-test the model behind the report. That's why I applied to ATU.

My employer knows I'm going. I intend to bring what I learn back. What happens after the degree — Bangladesh, Ireland, or somewhere else — I'll decide when I have real options, not now.
